Chapter 1 — How Solar Actually Works

Zero knowledge needed. Start here.

🌞 The Core Idea

Solar panels are made from silicon — a material found in sand. When sunlight strikes silicon, it knocks electrons loose and those moving electrons are electricity. No fuel, no moving parts, no burning anything. Just light hitting a surface and producing power.

Multiple panels are wired together to form an "array." A 10kW system on your home could easily offset your entire CEB bill and more — and earn you money on top.

💡 Real-World Analogy

Think of rain falling onto a sloped roof into gutters. Rain = sunlight. Roof = solar panels. Gutters = wires. The bigger the roof, the more rain you collect. The stronger the rain, the faster the buckets fill. Replace "rain" with sunlight and "water" with electricity — that's solar.

🏠 What Makes a Large Home Different

A large home with AC units and a water pump has two challenges smaller homes don't:

⚡ High surge current at startup

ACs and pumps need 2–3× their running power for the first 2–3 seconds when starting. Your inverter must handle this "surge" or it will trip.
📊 High total daily consumption

A 1-ton AC running 8 hrs = ~8 kWh/day alone. A water pump adds 1–3 kWh. Your system must be sized generously — typically 8–15kWp.
🌴 Giriulla Advantage

Giriulla sits at roughly 7°N in the Western Province — one of Sri Lanka's best solar zones. You get strong sun nearly year-round, with only slight reduction during the south-west monsoon (May–September). Even on cloudy days, panels still produce 50–70% of peak output.

Electricity Basics — 5 Words That Explain Everything

Master these and no solar salesman can confuse you.
🔴

Volt (V) — The Pressure

Forces electricity through a wire

Imagine water in a pipe. Voltage = the water pressure. More pressure = the water travels further and pushes harder. More voltage = electricity can power demanding devices across long cable runs.

Your panels produce DC at 12V, 24V, or 48V. Sri Lanka's grid and home appliances use 230V AC. The inverter bridges this gap.

💧

Ampere (A) — The Flow Rate

How much electricity is moving at once

If Volts = pressure, Amps = how wide the pipe is. A thick pipe flows more water; a thicker wire carries more current. Solar DC wires are thick (4–10mm²) because they carry high amperage.

Why it matters for you: Your 1-ton AC draws about 6–8A running, but spikes to 18–24A for 2–3 seconds on startup. Your inverter's surge rating must handle this, or it trips every time the AC starts.

⚡

Watt (W) — The Power Right Now

Volts × Amps = Watts

Watts = how much power is being consumed this moment. All appliances are rated in watts — it's how "hungry" they are.

💡 LED bulb — 9W
🌀 Fan — 60–75W
📺 TV — 80–120W
🧊 Fridge — 100–200W
❄️ 1-ton AC — 900–1100W
❄️ 1.5-ton AC — 1300–1600W
💧 Water pump (1HP) — 750–900W
🍳 Microwave — 800–1200W
🕐

Kilowatt-Hour (kWh) — Energy Used Over Time

This is the "unit" on your CEB bill

1 kWh = 1,000 watts running for 1 hour. Formula: kWh = Watts × Hours ÷ 1000

📖 Worked Example

Your 1,000W AC runs 8 hours/day: 1000 × 8 ÷ 1000 = 8 kWh/day = 8 units. At CEB's upper tariff (~Rs.75/unit), that's Rs.600/day just for one AC unit.

🔄

AC vs DC — Two Different Types of Electricity

Solar makes DC. Your home uses AC. The inverter translates.

DC (Direct Current) — electrons flow in one direction, like a river. Batteries and solar panels produce DC.

AC (Alternating Current) — electrons flip direction 50 times per second. Sri Lanka grid and home appliances: 230V AC.

⚠️ Critical for AC units and pumps

ACs and water pumps contain electric motors — "inductive loads." They require a pure sine wave inverter, not a cheap modified sine wave type. Cheap inverters cause motors to run hot, vibrate, and fail early. All quality hybrid inverters output pure sine wave — always confirm this spec.

Every Component — What It Does & What to Buy

6 components, explained simply with real Sri Lanka buying advice.
🟦

1. Solar Panels (PV Modules)

Convert sunlight → DC electricity

Each panel is silicon cells in an aluminium frame. For your large home: expect 16–28 panels depending on wattage.

Key specs: Watt-peak (Wp) — output under ideal conditions. Modern panels: 500–620Wp each. Efficiency — % of sunlight converted. Aim for 21%+. Temperature coefficient — Sri Lanka is hot. Better panels lose less output in heat; look for -0.35%/°C or better.

🛒 Buy in Sri Lanka

Tier 1 brands available locally: Jinko Solar, LONGi, Canadian Solar, AE Solar, Risen. Insist on 10-year product warranty + 25-year performance warranty (80% output guaranteed at year 25). Buy from SLSEA-registered dealers only.

🧠

2. Hybrid Inverter — The Brain

Manages panels + batteries + grid simultaneously

For your setup, this is the most critical (and expensive after panels) component. A hybrid inverter does 4 jobs at once:

⚡ Converts panel DC → 230V AC for your home
🔋 Charges batteries from panels (and from grid if needed)
🔌 Exports excess power to CEB grid and earns you money
🔦 During power cut: instantly switches to battery in milliseconds
⚠️ Critical — Surge Rating for your AC + Pump

A 1-ton AC (1,000W running) may surge to 3,000W at startup. A 1HP pump (750W) may surge to 2,200W. If both start close together plus household loads, your inverter must handle 8–10kW surge. Most 5kW hybrid inverters handle 10kW surge — always confirm the surge rating spec before buying.

Recommended brands (Sri Lanka, 2025): Deye, Growatt, Goodwe, Solis — all have local dealers, spare parts, and SLSEA approval. Must output pure sine wave.

For a large home with AC, Lithium LiFePO4 is strongly recommended. You'll likely need 10–20kWh capacity to cover critical loads (lights, fans, fridge — not AC) for 8–12 hours. Lead acid at that scale becomes extremely heavy, inefficient, and needs replacing in 4 years anyway.

🎯

4. MPPT Charge Controller (Built into your inverter)

Extracts maximum power from panels at all times

MPPT (Maximum Power Point Tracking) constantly adjusts to pull the maximum available power from your panels — like always driving in the optimal gear. In Sri Lanka's heat, panels can lose 10–15% without MPPT. It recovers most of this automatically.

Good news: all quality hybrid inverters have 1–2 built-in MPPT inputs. You don't need a separate unit. Confirm the inverter's MPPT voltage range covers your panel array's voltage.

🛡️

5. Wiring, Protection & Safety Devices

Never cut corners here — this keeps you safe

Solar DC Cable: UV-resistant, double-insulated, outdoor-rated. 4mm² or 6mm² copper. Never use regular household wire on the roof.

MC4 Connectors: Weatherproof click-lock connectors for panel cables. If ever joined by tape or twisting, reject the installation immediately.

DC Combiner + Fuses: Each panel string needs a fuse. Protects everything if a wire shorts.

AC MCB / Isolator: Between inverter and distribution board. Required by CEB for safe system isolation.

Surge Protection Device (SPD): Both DC side (panels) and AC side (inverter). Absorbs lightning-induced voltage spikes.

Lightning Arrestor: Critically important in Sri Lanka. Directs a strike safely to ground, away from your expensive inverter and panels.

Earth Bonding: All metal frames, inverter chassis, battery cabinet connected to an earth rod buried in the ground.

⚡

Safety rule you must never forget: Solar panels produce voltage whenever light hits them — even on a cloudy day, even at dawn. You cannot switch them off. Before anyone touches wiring, panels must be covered with a thick dark tarpaulin or cardboard.

📊

6. Bidirectional / Net Meter

CEB installs this — you don't buy it

CEB replaces your current meter with one that tracks both directions: units you take from the grid (you pay) and units you send to the grid (you earn). This is installed free by CEB after your system is approved and the agreement is signed.

AC Units & Water Pumps — Special Considerations

The trickiest loads in any solar system. Here's exactly what you must know.
❄️

Air Conditioners on Solar

Your biggest single load — manageable with correct sizing

Can solar run an AC? Absolutely yes — millions of homes do it. The keys are correct inverter sizing and understanding when to run the AC.

0.75 ton (9,000 BTU)600–750W1,500–2,000W2–3 panels (600Wp)

1.5 ton (18,000 BTU)1,300–1,600W3,500–4,500W4–6 panels

⚠️ Inverter surge rating is critical

The startup surge lasts only 2–3 seconds but it must not exceed your inverter's surge rating. If you have 2 ACs, confirm they can't both start simultaneously (staggered AC start timers are built into some hybrid inverters).

💡 Pro Tip — Buy an Inverter-Type AC

If buying a new AC, choose an inverter-type (variable speed) AC — Panasonic, Mitsubishi, LG Dual Inverter etc. These have far lower startup surges (sometimes just 1.3× running) and are 30–40% more energy efficient. Perfect pairing with solar.

💧

Water Pumps on Solar

High surge, short runtime — very manageable

Pumps are motor loads with large startup surges. But they typically only run for 30 mins–2 hrs/day, making their daily energy use quite manageable.

💡 Smart Strategy — Schedule Your Pump

Programme the pump to run between 10am–2pm when panels are at peak output. It runs directly off solar with no battery use, keeping batteries fully charged for night backup. Most hybrid inverter apps let you set smart load schedules.

🧠

The "Total Simultaneous Load" Rule: Your inverter continuous rating must exceed everything running at the same time. Example: 1-ton AC (1kW) + water pump (0.75kW) + fridge (0.15kW) + lights/fans (0.5kW) = 2.4kW simultaneous. A 5kW inverter handles this comfortably. But the surge when AC + pump both start within seconds of each other could hit 6kW — so the inverter's surge rating matters just as much as continuous rating.

Sri Lanka Grid Connection & Earning Money

SLSEA, CEB, Net Metering, and the Soorya Bala Sangramaya program.

🏛️ The 3 Authorities You'll Deal With

SLSEA — Sri Lanka Sustainable Energy Authority
Registers and certifies solar installers. Sets technical standards. You must use an SLSEA-registered company or CEB will not approve your grid connection. Verify at energy.gov.lk.
CEB — Ceylon Electricity Board
Your distributor in Giriulla. Approves your system, inspects installation, installs the net meter, and pays you for exported energy. PUCSL mandates they must respond within 2 weeks of application.
PUCSL — Public Utilities Commission
Consumer regulator. They've granted all domestic consumers the legal right to install solar and earn from it — no generation license needed for home systems.

💰 3 Earning Schemes — Compared

SchemeHow It WorksBest For
Net MeteringMeter spins forward importing, backward exporting. Pay only the net difference each month.Homes using most power during daytime

Net Plus ★ (Soorya Bala Sangramaya)Two separate meters. CEB pays Rs.22/unit for ALL generated units (yrs 1–7), then Rs.15.50/unit. You pay normal tariff for consumption separately.Large systems — maximizes income
🏠 Best for your large home

With a large system (8–12kWp), the Net Plus scheme will likely earn the most. At 10kWp generating ~1,650 units/month, you'd earn roughly Rs.36,300/month from CEB in years 1–7 — while separately managing your consumption bill.

Installation — What to Prepare & Watch For

Be an informed homeowner, not just a bystander.

🏠 Roof Checklist for a Large System

✅ Unshaded area: For 8–12kWp you need 40–60m² (each 600Wp panel ≈ 2.5m²). Consider trimming trees that shade between 9am–3pm.
✅ Orientation: South-facing = maximum. An East+West split gives a flatter generation curve through the day — good if you have panels on both sides.
✅ Roof pitch: 10–15° is ideal. Flat concrete roofs need tilt frames (add Rs.5,000–10,000 per panel).
✅ Structural load: 20 panels × 25kg = ~500kg extra. Ask a civil engineer or trusted builder to confirm your roof can handle this.
✅ Roof material: Clay tiles, concrete, GI sheets, asbestos — each has specific mounting methods. Asbestos is fragile — inform the installer in advance.

🔌 Equipment Placement

Hybrid Inverter: Wall-mounted indoors, shaded, well-ventilated. Within 5m of main distribution board. Open garage wall or equipment room is ideal. Never in a sealed room — inverters generate heat.
Battery bank: Ground level (batteries are heavy). Lithium needs minimal ventilation. Lead acid needs its own ventilated room. Keep away from heat sources and direct sun.
DC combiner/fuse box: Near the roof cable entry point — keeps DC cable runs short and safe.
Main AC disconnect: Between inverter and distribution board. Must be accessible at all times.

🧹 Ongoing Maintenance (Minimal)

🌧️ Panel cleaning every 4–8 weeks. Dust, bird droppings, and coconut pollen reduce output by 10–20%. Soft cloth + water in early morning. No abrasive cleaners.
🔍 Visual check every 6 months — cracked panels, loose MC4 connectors, any wire discoloration.
📱 Monitor your inverter app daily. If generation is significantly lower than expected on a sunny day, something needs checking. Modern inverters send automatic alerts.
🔋 Lead acid only: Check electrolyte level monthly, top up with distilled water.

Test Your Knowledge — 8 Questions

Tailored to your exact setup: hybrid system, large home, AC and pump loads.
1. Your 1.5-ton AC (1,400W running) starts up. The startup surge is approximately:
Motor loads surge to 2.5–3× running watts at startup. Your hybrid inverter's surge rating must handle this — not just the continuous rating.
2. Your 1HP water pump (750W) runs 2 hours daily. Monthly energy consumption is:
750W × 2hrs = 1,500Wh = 1.5 kWh/day. × 30 days = 45 kWh/month. Schedule it during peak solar (10am–2pm) and it costs you nothing from the grid.
3. During a CEB power cut at 9pm, your hybrid inverter with a charged battery will:
This is the hybrid system's biggest advantage over on-grid. The switchover is essentially instantaneous. Your lights, fridge, fans stay on. Even ACs keep running if the battery is large enough.
4. Why must you use an SLSEA-registered solar installer?
Using an unregistered company means CEB cannot legally connect your system to the grid. You lose the ability to earn from exports and could face disconnection. Check energy.gov.lk for the registered list.
5. Under the Soorya Bala Sangramaya Net Plus scheme, how much does CEB pay per unit generated in years 1–7?
Rs.22/unit for years 1–7, then Rs.15.50/unit from year 8. For a 10kWp system generating ~1,650 units/month, that's Rs.36,300/month income in the first 7 years from exports alone.
6. Why is Lithium LiFePO4 better long-term value than lead acid despite higher upfront cost?
Over 15 years you'd replace lead acid 3 times at similar total cost. Lithium's deeper usable depth means you need less installed capacity too. For a large home, lithium almost always wins on total cost of ownership.
7. Why must your hybrid inverter output "pure sine wave" for your AC and water pump?
Electric motors (in ACs, pumps, washing machines) require clean AC power. Modified sine wave inverters cause motors to run hotter, vibrate more, and can damage compressors over time. All reputable hybrid inverters output pure sine wave.
8. The smartest time to run your water pump to save battery backup is:
Running high-wattage loads during peak solar means they draw directly from panel output — not from batteries. This preserves your full battery charge for nighttime backup and extends battery lifespan significantly.
